Abstract

Bangladesh is currently dealing with energy production problems characterized by recurrent load shedding and total reliance on foreign fossil fuel imports. In order to ensure a consistent and dependable supply of electricity constantly, the HAYTHAM X ONE project offers the idea of using an innovative integrated approach that combines cutting-edge Concentrating Solar Power (CSP) technology with effective energy storage systems supervised by Artificial Intelligence (AI) driven management while utilizing Bangladesh's geographic and environmental potential.

This approach optimizes operational efficiency and minimizes energy loss, positioning Bangladesh as a green energy leader in the region. The effectiveness of renewable energy sources is largely dependent on favorable weather. To counter this issue, our proposed framework combines CSP, various redundant independent energy storage systems such as Reverse Electrodialysis (RED) batteries, sand thermal storage and others in response to Bangladesh's energy needs and climate. Additionally, this paper explores the production of hydrogen as a renewable fuel source.

Key Technologies

Concentrating Solar Power

Advanced heliostat systems concentrate sunlight onto receivers, generating superheated steam at 1500°C to power turbines. Click to learn more.

Sand Thermal Storage

Cost-effective thermal energy storage using readily available sand with 80% efficiency. Single-tank thermocline design for long-duration storage.

Reverse Electrodialysis

Innovative energy storage utilizing salinity gradients between freshwater and saltwater through ion-exchange membranes.

Hydrogen Production

Thermo-Chemical Water Splitting Process produces clean hydrogen fuel using excess thermal energy at 800-1200°C.

Hydrogen Fuel Cells

Proton Exchange Membrane Fuel Cells convert stored hydrogen back into electricity with over 60% efficiency.

AI-Driven Management

Reinforcement Learning, Deep Neural Networks, and Predictive Analytics optimize system performance in real-time.

Methodology

System Integration

Centralized control synchronizes heliostats, PTRs, CSP towers, molten salt and sand storage, RED stacks and hydrogen production for continuous coastal grid power.

Water Processing

Seawater filtration and Helio-Thermal Brine Separation using Parabolic Trough Reflectors produce freshwater for the process and brine for RED batteries.

Storage Hierarchy

Primary sand thermocline for long-duration heat. Secondary hydrogen production via TCWSP and RED units for emergency and long-term backup.

AI Optimization

RL for heliostat tracking, DNNs for forecasting irradiance and demand, predictive analytics to reduce downtime and extend equipment life by 30-50%.
